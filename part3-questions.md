## Q1 (6 pts) — Forces on a block
4 kg block on a **frictionless** surface. F₁ = 18 N (right), F₂ = 6 N (left).
FN and Fg cancel (vertical).
- a) Calculate net force Fnet on the block. (2 pts)
- b) Calculate the acceleration of the block. (2 pts)
- c) Are the forces balanced or unbalanced? Justify in one sentence. (2 pts)

## Q2 (6 pts) — Suitcase in an elevator
25 kg suitcase rests on the floor of an elevator. g = 10 m/s².
- a) Find the weight Fg of the suitcase. (3 pts)
- b) If the elevator is at rest, find the normal force Fn from the floor. (3 pts)

## Q3 (5 pts) — Worker pulls a toolbox
Worker pulls a toolbox 20 m across a horizontal floor with a constant
horizontal pull of 50 N. A constant friction force of 15 N opposes the motion.
- a) Calculate the work done by the worker's pull on the toolbox. (2 pts)
- b) Calculate the work done by friction on the toolbox. (2 pts)
- c) Calculate the net work done on the toolbox. (1 pt)

## Q4 (6 pts) — Work–energy theorem
3 kg object initially moves at 4 m/s. A net force does +30 J of net work on it
along the direction of motion.
- a) Calculate the initial kinetic energy KEᵢ. (3 pts)
- b) Using Wnet = ΔKE, calculate the final kinetic energy KE_f. (3 pts)

## Q5 (6 pts) — Energy of a falling ball
0.5 kg ball released from rest at height 5 m above the ground. Air resistance
negligible. g = 10 m/s², ground at h = 0.
- a) Calculate the PEg at the release point and state the KE at release. (3 pts)
- b) Calculate the total mechanical energy ME at the release point. (3 pts)

## Q6 (5 pts) — Momentum of a ball
2 kg ball moves to the right at +8 m/s. Right is positive.
- a) Calculate the momentum p of the ball. (2 pts)
- b) If the ball is now moving to the left at −8 m/s with the same speed,
     calculate its new momentum. (2 pts)
- c) State the change in momentum Δp between the two situations. (1 pt)

## Q7 (6 pts) — Impulse on a cart
Constant force of 40 N acts on a 5 kg cart, initially at rest on a frictionless
surface, for a time interval Δt = 0.5 s.
- a) Calculate the impulse J delivered to the cart. (2 pts)
- b) Calculate the initial momentum pᵢ of the cart. (2 pts)
- c) Using J = Δp, calculate the final momentum p_f of the cart. (2 pts)

## Q8 (6 pts) — Baseball struck by a bat
0.15 kg baseball approaches a bat at +30 m/s. After being struck, it moves in
the opposite direction at −20 m/s.
- a) Calculate the initial momentum pᵢ and the final momentum p_f of the ball. (2 pts)
- b) Calculate the change in momentum Δp of the ball. (2 pts)
- c) If the contact time is Δt = 0.02 s, calculate the force F exerted by the bat
     on the ball. (2 pts)

## Q9 (7 pts) — Two skaters push off (conservation of momentum)
60 kg skater and 40 kg skater stand at rest on frictionless ice. They push off
each other. Immediately after the push, the 60 kg skater moves left at −2 m/s.
(Left is negative, right is positive.)
- a) Calculate the total momentum of the two-skater system before the push and
     justify. (2 pts)
- b) Using conservation of momentum, find the velocity of the 40 kg skater
     immediately after the push (mention the direction too). (5 pts)

## Q10 (7 pts) — Car collision (perfectly inelastic)
Car A (m₁ = 1200 kg) travels east at +25 m/s and collides with stationary
Car B (m₂ = 1800 kg). The two cars lock together after impact (perfectly
inelastic). Take east as positive.
- a) Calculate the total momentum of the system before the collision. (2 pts)
- b) Find the common velocity v_f of the two cars immediately after the
     collision. (5 pts)

## Bonus Question (3 pts — Extra Credit) — Braking with impulse
A 1500 kg car is traveling at +20 m/s. A constant braking force of −300 N is
applied. Using impulse, calculate how long it takes the brakes to bring the car
to rest.
